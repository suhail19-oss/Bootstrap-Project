Frequently Asked Questions...

Some quick answers to your questions

An eBook, short for electronic book, is a digital version of a printed book that can be read on electronic devices such as e-readers, tablets, smartphones, or computers.

eBooks are typically available in formats such as EPUB, MOBI, PDF, and AZW (Amazon's proprietary format). EPUB is a widely used format compatible with most e-readers and apps, while MOBI is primarily used by Amazon Kindle devices.

No, you don't necessarily need an e-reader. eBooks can be read on various devices using dedicated reading apps or software. However, e-readers offer a specialized reading experience optimized for extended reading sessions.

Many eBook reading apps and devices allow users to highlight text, add annotations, and bookmark pages, similar to marking up a physical book. These features enhance the reading experience and facilitate note-taking and studying.

Like printed books, eBooks are protected by copyright law. Distributing or reproducing eBooks without permission from the copyright holder may constitute copyright infringement. However, fair use exceptions and licensing agreements may allow limited use for purposes such as education, research, or criticism. Always respect copyright laws when accessing and sharing eBooks.
